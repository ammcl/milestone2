A Trip of a Life Time

For two months, between December 2017 and January 2018, I embarked on a trip of a lifetime. I hope you enjoy a sample of my trip to a wonderful country.

Suggested Tours

There are so many different tours to take in and around Cape Town, you have the Big Red Bus which is a must to take. However if you wish to explore some alternatives then I would highly recommend the following companies.

Experience The Forgotten Route

The true story of a forgotten route, following in the footsteps of early explorers into the wild undiscovered, REAL South Africa. It’s now a journey that you can experience…The Forgotten Route

Wine Country

With so many wine farms to choose from that if you were to visit them all, by the end of the trip you would definately need to seek out medical attention. This is why I'm recommending a nice quiet vineyard situated in the picturesque Breedekloof Valley (Rawsonville), less than one hour's drive from Cape Town. Adventure

National Parks

Mount Zebra

Mountain Zebra National Park is a national park in the Eastern Cape province of South Africa proclaimed in July 1937 for the purpose of providing a nature reserve for the endangered Cape mountain zebra. In the early 1930s, the Cape mountain zebra was threatened with extinction.

Did you know that not all Zebras have the same pattern of stripes :-).

Mount Zebra National Park

Addo

Addo Elephant National Park is a diverse wildlife conservation park situated close to Port Elizabeth in South Africa and is one of the country's 19 national parks. It currently ranks third in size after Kruger National Park and the Kgalagadi Transfrontier Park.

Where size doesn't matter, you have to stop even for a Dung Beetle!

Addo Elepant National Park

Camdeboo

Formed hundreds of millions of years ago, the Karoo of South Africa is one of the great natural wonders of the world. Camdeboo National Park provides the visitor with insights into the unique landscape and ecosystem of the Karoo, not to mention awesome scenic beauty
